Scenario

Structural Sound is a precision electronics company that produces innovative, high-end speakers, headphones, and other quality digital audio products at its popular stand-alone stores located in big cities and shopping malls across the United States.


For years, all employees at headquarters have been organized into job-specific (functional) departments, such as accounting, engineering, marketing, manufacturing, and so forth. The executive board believes that the company would benefit from reorganizing utilizing product departmentalization, in which employees will be grouped based upon product lines. Each product line (such as speakers) will operate autonomously and will have specialists in all of the functions needed to produce and sell that product, such as marketing, manufacturing, accounting, and so on.


Earlier this month, the board sent a memo to all employees announcing this new organizational structure and detailing the reasons for the change, including:

  1. Decreased product development time
  2. Better utilization of human resources through specialization
  3. More teamwork and better communication among groups of employees focused on a specific product line

Employees are awaiting details on how the new structure will affect them. At the request of Marissa Thielen, Director of Human Resources, you must prepare and deliver a presentation giving employees the details they need and explaining how the organizational change will affect them.


Decision Point: What's Your Presentation's Purpose?

You have a planning meeting with Marissa. She tells you that she recommends the advice of leadership expert Stephen Covey: "Begin with the end in mind," meaning that good presentations have both a specific purpose and a specific intended outcome.

"If you want your audience to know something, inform them," she says. "If you want them to believe or do something, persuade them. If you want them to know how to do something, instruct them."

Based on Marissa's advice, which of the following best describes the purpose of your presentation?

The purpose of your presentation is to inform the employees about the details of the new organizational structure.

The purpose of your presentation is to not only inform employees about the details of the new organizational structure, but also to emphasize the benefits of the reorganization for both them and the company.

The purpose of your presentation is to inform the employees about the details of the new organizational structure and conduct training in best practices of product departmentalization.
